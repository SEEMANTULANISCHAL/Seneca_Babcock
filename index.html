From Seneca Nation Hunting Grounds to Thriving Community

150+ years of neighborhood evolution documented through historical research and community stories

The Seneca Babcock area has a rich history spanning centuries, from its origins as hunting and fishing grounds for the Seneca Nation, through its transformation during Buffalo's industrial boom, to today's resilient community working toward revitalization.

Our neighborhood has weathered significant challenges including the construction of the Thruway in the 1950s, but continues to thrive through the dedication of longtime residents and community organizations.
